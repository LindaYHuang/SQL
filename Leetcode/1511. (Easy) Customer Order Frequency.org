SQL Schema

+---------------+---------+
order_id is the primary key for this table.

customer_id is the id of the customer who bought "quantity" products with id "product_id".

Write an SQL query to report the customer_id and customer_name of customers who have spent at least $100 in each month of June and July 2020.

Return the result table in any order.

The query result format is in the following example.

 
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
Customers
+--------------+-----------+-------------+
| customer_id  | name      | country     |
+--------------+-----------+-------------+
| 1            | Winston   | USA         |
| 2            | Jonathan  | Peru        |
| 3            | Moustafa  | Egypt       |
+--------------+-----------+-------------+

Product
+--------------+-------------+-------------+
| product_id   | description | price       |
+--------------+-------------+-------------+
| 10           | LC Phone    | 300         |
| 20           | LC T-Shirt  | 10          |
| 30           | LC Book     | 45          |
| 40           | LC Keychain | 2           |
+--------------+-------------+-------------+

Orders
+--------------+-------------+-------------+-------------+-----------+
| order_id     | customer_id | product_id  | order_date  | quantity  |
+--------------+-------------+-------------+-------------+-----------+
| 1            | 1           | 10          | 2020-06-10  | 1         |
| 2            | 1           | 20          | 2020-07-01  | 1         |
| 3            | 1           | 30          | 2020-07-08  | 2         |
| 4            | 2           | 10          | 2020-06-15  | 2         |
| 5            | 2           | 40          | 2020-07-01  | 10        |
| 6            | 3           | 20          | 2020-06-24  | 2         |
| 7            | 3           | 30          | 2020-06-25  | 2         |
| 9            | 3           | 30          | 2020-05-08  | 3         |
+--------------+-------------+-------------+-------------+-----------+
#+END_EXAMPLE

Result table:
#+BEGIN_EXAMPLE
+--------------+------------+
| customer_id  | name       |  
+--------------+------------+
| 1            | Winston    |
+--------------+------------+ 
Winston spent $300 (300 * 1) in June and $100 ( 10 * 1 + 45 * 2) in July 2020.
Jonathan spent $600 (300 * 2) in June and $20 ( 2 * 10) in July 2020.
Moustafa spent $110 (10 * 2 + 45 * 2) in June and $0 in July 2020.
#+END_EXAMPLE



---------------------------------------------------------------------
- Solution:

#+BEGIN_SRC sql
SELECT t2.customer_id
,c.name
FROM 
    (SELECT t1.customer_id, t1.month, SUM(t1.quantity*t1.price) as monthly_order_price
        (Select o.customer_id, o.quantity, p.price, DATEPART(MONTH,o.order_date) as month
         FROM Orders o 
         INNER JOIN Product p ON (o.product_id = p.product_id)  
         WHERE month in (6,7)) as t1
    GROUP BY t1.customer_id, t1. month
    HAVING monthly_order_price >100) as t2
INNER JOIN Customers c ON t2.customer_id = c.customer_id
#+END_SRC
